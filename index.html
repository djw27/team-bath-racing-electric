About The Project

Team Bath Racing Electric is a student run project at the University of Bath. Now in it's second year of running the project is gaining traction fast with interest growing from within the University and within Industry.

TBRe hopes to show that electric racing cars competing within Formula Student can be designed and built in a sustainable way which does not impact on overall car performance. To help demonstrate this, the project makes use of second hand components where possible and undergoes a full life-cycle analysis at every stage of development. By using an old Team Bath Racing Formula Student car chasis (TBR12) and using Engineers from a range of backgrounds within the University to re-package a custom designed electric powertrain, we hope to have a working proof-of-concept vehicle in 2014/2015.

Our Goals

Team Bath Racing Electric was setup by students, for students. The main goal of the project is to design and build a fully electric car for competition in Formula Student events. With this in mind, our initial aim is to build a car which can competitively compete with the Team Bath Racing petrol team.

TBRe helps develop the skills of students involved into a set which is relevant for the future and applicable to cross-discipline industry roles.

Students

Students are the life and soul of projects at any University.

With TBRe we offer students the opportunity to develop their real world Engineering skills by working as part of a dynamic team in designing and creating a novel product throught the use of original ideas.

Sponsors

Working closely with students and helping to ensure the future viability of the project helps the University develop industry leading graduate Engineers.

TBRe aims to work closely with industrial partners in order to ensure this continues to happen.
